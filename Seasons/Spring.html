SPRING

Spring marks the transition from the cold and dreary winter to a fresh and vibrant new season. As the sun gradually becomes stronger and the days grow longer, nature starts to awaken from its slumber. Trees and flowers begin to bloom, filling the landscape with an array of colors and fragrances. The air becomes filled with a sense of rejuvenation, and animals emerge from hibernation to forage for food and start their mating rituals. Spring is a season of hope and new beginnings, symbolizing growth, rebirth, and the promise of warmer days ahead.

    FACTS

  • Spring is often associated with growth, new beginnings, and rejuvenation.
  • It occurs between March and May in the Northern Hemisphere and between September and November in the Southern Hemisphere.
  • The word "spring" comes from the Old English word "springan" which means "to leap up" or "to burst forth."
  • During spring, the temperatures gradually rise and the days become longer, leading to the blossoming of flowers and the return of many animals from hibernation.
  • Spring is also known for its unpredictable weather, including rain showers, thunderstorms, and rapidly changing temperatures.
